Cancer

A disease caused by mutations in cellular growth factor proteins such as RAS and P53, is a major cause of death worldwide. Oncogenesis not only occurs in many different parts of the body, but the problem of cancer locality is exacerbated by many variants of cancerâ€™s ability to metastasize, which is a process by which cancerous cells migrate throughout the body. Metastasis is caused in part by mutations in cell adhesion proteins such as E-Cadherin. This process of metastasis renders many cancer treatments difficult or impractical, as the target of treatment no longer remains localized. Therefore, it is key to correctly identify and localize the source of cancer early in development, as to prepare the most precise and effective treatments.

Leukemia

One of the major forms of cancer currently affecting children is leukemia. Leukemia is a type of cancer which affects blood cells, causing them to develop either prematurely (Acute), or not go through apoptosis (Chronic). In further specificity, leukemia can be broken into two types: One which primarily affects white blood cells (lymphoblastic), and another which primarily affects red blood cells (myeloid). Due to the wide subtypes of leukemia, treatment effectiveness can maximized when the proper form of leukemia is identified.
